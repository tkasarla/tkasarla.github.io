abstract: >
  We study the relationship between hyperbolic geometry and out-of-distribution (OOD)
  detection. We show that when embeddings in hyperbolic space are balanced — i.e., they
  respect the natural hierarchical structure of the space — in-distribution and OOD samples
  separate geometrically without any explicit OOD supervision. The norm of a hyperbolic
  embedding encodes certainty: in-distribution samples concentrate near the boundary while
  OOD samples drift toward the origin. This provides a principled, geometry-driven
  foundation for OOD detection in visual recognition models, requiring no post-hoc scoring
  or additional training objectives.
